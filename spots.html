Best Tourist Spots in El Nido

Secret Lagoon

It’s no secret that El Nido has been blessed with more than its fair share of postcard perfect scenic locations. From majestic limestone formation jutting out from crystal clear turquoise seas to powdery white sandy beaches along its coastlines, not to mention the sheer diversity of marine wildlife in its perfectly hued oceans, its no wonder foreign publications have declared it the best beach and island destination in the Philippines.

Las Canas Beach

Just a few hundred meters away from Marimegmeg Beach is the Las Cabanas Beach. From here, if the tide is low you can take a walk to the Depeldet Island or, if you’re feeling adventurous, you can try the zip line.

Hidden Beach

Hidden Beach is known to be a haven for the endangered clownfish, which is made popular by the animated film Finding Nemo. Your tour guides can show you where to spot them. There are around three seaweed havens where you can find these fishes.

Nacpan Beach

Nacpan Beach is a four-kilometer strip of cream-colored sand and coconut trees with crystal clear turquoise water. It is located 17th kilometers north of the El Nido town proper in Palawan. This powdery paradise was one of the secret getaways for some tourists until its beauty was discovered by many and eventually became one of the most popular tourist spots in El Nido.

Taraw Cliff

The Taraw Cliff is the most impressive mountain in El Nido in the Philippines and climbing Taraw Cliff is one of the coolest things to do and the most awesome hike you can do in El Nido. Whereas hiking is a bit of an understatement, because you’re actually climbing the mountain. What you will experience during the hike on the Taraw Cliff and if it is dangerous or not we will tell you in our travel report

Lio Beach

Bounded by the beach on one side and the mountains on the other, one cannot ask for a more beautiful piece of land than Lio, in El Nido, Palawan, a place renowned for its pristine beaches, diverse wildlife and breathtaking vistas. Indulge in unique shopping and dining experiences offered for your convenience and set against a picturesque backdrop. Sample local and international fare or treat yourself to locally made products and handicrafts.

Big Lagoon

As you enter the Big Lagoon, everything is a sight to behold. You'll be greeted by the crystal-clear blue water, the ray of light peeking through the top of the cliffs, and the luscious calcareous formation. The Big Lagoon of El Nido island hopping tour A has become popular for its breathtaking natural structure as seen from drone shots to kayak views in several movies and postcards.

Small Lagoon

El Nido's Small Lagoon, located in the Philippines, is a hidden gem that offers a unique and tranquil experience for its visitors. Nestled within towering limestone cliffs on Miniloc Island, the Small Lagoon is a part of the stunning El Nido archipelago, a renowned area for its natural beauty and biodiversity. The lagoon is accessible by a small break in the cliffs, which can be traversed by kayak or swimming during low tide.
